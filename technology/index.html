Rationale

Life is one with nature, built by eons of contextual experience and patterns that became part of all organisms: to maintain control, living things must embed in their structure the dynamics of the inputs they are subjected to.

Thus predicting the body's real time metabolic state becomes a problem of mathematical modeling and system identification, coupled with proper knowledge of the dynamics of the system's inputs.

Glucose is the fuel of life, and everything we eat or drink converts to glucose molecules that are transported to each and every cell in the body so that it has the energy needed to do its work: by gaining access to the overlapping cycles of intake, storage, and consumption of glucose processes that occur in the living body, it now becomes possible to reduce or even eliminate out-of-control situations that stress the body and cause premature living system failure situations, such as obesity, diabetes, and other.
