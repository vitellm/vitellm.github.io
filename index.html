A Theorem is a Love Letter Written to all Like-Minded People of the World... Now and Forever

Theorem: A system with feedforward will be stable only if its structure contains elements that have dynamic characteristics that are 'close enough' to those of the feedforward input(s).

Several corollaries exist, the three most relevant being that a) any live organism is a feedforward system that through evolution has become hyperstable; b) if the time functions for the physical variables that a living system is subjected to are known, the structure of the system itself can be determined 'close enough' from only a few measurements of the output variable; c) a living organism's response to any input provided by its natural environment is predictable; and d) loss of stability to any exogenous perturbation results in premature death.
